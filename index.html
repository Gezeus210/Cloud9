Helpful Tips

-When first starting the hobby or career of photography, think about what you want to capture

-When taking some shots, always remember quality over quantity

-Try developing your own style that best suits you, instead of trying to force yourself to take another style. Everyone has their strengths and weaknesses

-Try to get engaged or interested with your shot/photograph.

-A good picture does not come fast, take your time with shots and be patient.

Definitions of Terms

-A Light Sensor is responsible for taking the light in and producing an image from it.

-Aperture is the term which refers to the cameras lens diameter which can affect how much light enters the camera and the focus of the final product.

-Shutter Speed is the term which refers to the speed a camera's shutter opens, which affects how the "motion" of the picture looks.

-ISO refers to the film's or sensor's sensitivity to light, changing it can cause an image to come out darker or brighter.

What is Photogrpahy

Photography is the capturing light through a camera light sensor to produce an image.


Quick History

During the year 1827, a french scientist named Joseph Nicephore Niepce created the first "photograph" by engraving a metal plate, coating it with bitumen (asphalt) and then exposing it to light. The timeline for photography started with the first picture which was captured in 1827, kodak then began selling the first commercially used camera in 1888. WW2 introduced a new style of photography which in turn opened doors for more styles to be developed. Polaroid in 1948 introduced instant image development and finally the first professional digital camera is developed in 1991.


When Photography Changed

During WW2, there was an introduction of wartime photos which brought forth a new "style" of photography, thus opening a door for new styles to emerge in the field. The field was expanded even further during the 1990s when the first professional digital camera was invented for everyone to use.
