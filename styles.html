Tips for Each Style

Macro- Zooming in can cause a loss in picture quality, try to get as physically close as you can to your shot before using zoom.


Aerial- When taking an aerial shot, always take into account the time of day your planning on and the weather conditions of the area.


Landscape- Wide lens is a pretty easy thing to keep in mind when photographing an entire landscape, but also remember to try and keep everything in focus to create some depth to your shot.


Portrait- Don't get to lost in getting just your subject right, the background of the photo also matters.


Forced Perspective- With forcing a perspective two things that can really be a help, are seeking the right location and asking a partner to tag along.


How to Find Your Photography Style

Different Styles

Macro Photography

-Macro photography is an extreme close up of the subject


Aerial Photography

-Aerial Photography is pretty direct also, just some shots of a subject but from an aerial view


Landscape Photography

-Landscape photography is the capturing of spaces whether it be nature or man-made, as long as the picture captures an environment


Portrait Photography

-Portrait photography is when a picture is trying to capture a subjects personality or essence with the use of color, background, and poses


Forced Perspective

-Forced perspective is when the photo "forces" us to view something in a different view. For example, viewing something we know is gigantic but the picture makes it seem small
